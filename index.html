Gender Equality Awareness

"Breaking barriers, building equality"

SDG5:   GENDER EQUALITY

SDG 5 is short for Sustainable Development Goal 5, set by the United Nations in 2015. It aims to achieve gender equality, meaning equal opportunities and rights for all women and girls. This includes ending discrimination and violence, ensuring access to education and healthcare, and empowering women to take leadership roles. It's a crucial goal for building a peaceful, prosperous, and sustainable world.


It's one of 17 SDGs established by the United Nations in 2015. These goals recognize that progress in one area impacts others, and achieving sustainability requires balance between social, economic, and environmental aspects.

NOTABLE MILESTONES IN GENDER EQUALITY

1900

MARRIED WOMEN'S PROPERTY ACTS IN EVERY STATE

By 1900, all states in the U.S. had passed Married Women’s Property Acts which gave married women the right to own property and keep their earnings from their own work.

1920

WHITE WOMAN ALLOWED TO VOTE

White women allowed to vote: The 19th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, ratified in 1920, prohibited states and the federal government from denying the right to vote on the basis of sex.

1944

THE WOMEN'S ARMY AUXILIARY CORPS FORMED BY THE U.S ARMY

The WAAC allowed women to serve their country in a way that had not been previously possible. Over 150,000 women served in the WAAC during the war.

1963

THE EQUAL PAY ACT

It requires that men and women in the same establishment who perform equal work under similar working conditions receive equal pay.

1969

FIRST "NO FAULT" DIVORCE LAW ADOPTED BY CALIFORNIA

Prior to no-fault divorce laws, one spouse had to prove the other spouse was at fault in order to get a divorce. This change made it easier and less contentious to end a marriage.

1973

SUPREME COURT RULING IN ROE V. WADE MADE ABORTION LEGAL

The Court ruled that a woman has a constitutional right to an abortion without excessive government restriction in the early stages of pregnancy.

2023

TEXAS SUPREME COURT STRIKES DOWN STATE LAWS THAT MAKE GAY SEX A CRIME

In Lawrence v. Texas, Supreme Court strikes down state laws that make gay sex a crime: In 2003, the Supreme Court struck down state laws that criminalized sex between same-sex couples. The Court’s decision in Lawrence v. Texas was a major victory for LGBTQ+ rights.

2021

KAMALA HARRIES BECOMES THE FIRST WOMAN (AND BLACK AND ASIAN) VP

Kamala Harris becomes first woman (and Black and Asian) VP: In 2021, Kamala Harris was inaugurated as the first female, Black, and Asian-American vice president of the United States.

THINGS TO DO

Women earn 10 to 30 per cent less than men for the same work. Pay inequality persists everywhere. Voice your support for equal pay for equal work.

Be aware of gender stereotypes. Recognize them, avoid them and educate others about them.

Find female mentors/leaders. Ensure that some of your role models or mentors are women. There’s a lot you can learn from women in positions of authority.

Find a Goal 5 charity you want to support. Any donation, big or small, can make a difference!

Stand up against harassment. Whenever you see or become aware of harassment, whether in the workplace, streets, home or the online space, raise your voice against it.

Share the workload at home. Sharing domestic responsibilities ensures the work burden doesn’t fall only on one person and instills the value of gender equality and essential life skills in children.
